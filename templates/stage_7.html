Day 3 ‚Äì Deeksha Tithi (Initiation Day)

This is a sacred and subtle turning point. Bhairava's initiation is not theatrical‚Äîit is mystical, silent, and powerful. The mantra becomes a living force inside you.

Initiation Day Components

1 Sacred Bath (SnƒÅna Vidhi)
  • Take a ritual bath before sunrise.
  • Mix turmeric, black sesame seeds, and vibhuti in the water.
2 Altar Preparation (Puja Mandala)
  • Prepare a simple altar with Bhairava's image, yantra, or Shiva Linga.
  • Offer sesame oil or ghee lamp, black mustard seeds, red flowers, incense, and a piece of iron or black cloth.

  • Chant Bhairava Dhyana Sloka (provided).
  • Perform a short avahana (invocation) and mentally offer:

4 Mantra Transmission
"Om Bhairavaya Namaha"
  • Chant minimum 108 times (or 1008, if possible).
  • Let each repetition carry intensity, emotion, and presence.
5 Establishing BhƒÅva (Inner Orientation)
  • Sit in silence. Visualize:
    • Bhairava in the heart center, or
    • Blazing light in the Ajna Chakra (third eye).
  • Allow feelings of fearlessness, clarity, and divine guidance to arise.
6 Sealing the Connection
  • Offer a simple Naivedya (milk, fruit, etc.).
  • Inwardly say:
"Let this link be eternal. Let Bhairava walk with me, in me, as me."
